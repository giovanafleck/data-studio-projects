Published July 31, 2019

According to data provided by the New York City police department, on average, there were 1,098 deaths each year due to unintentional motor vehicle traffic-related injuries, killing 5.6 of every 100,000 New Yorkers. The rates were highest for males and New Yorkers ages 65 - including pedestrians, cyclists and drivers.

The rate of deaths due to unintentional motor vehicle traffic-related injuries decreased from a high of 8.4 per 100,000 residents in 2001, to a low of 4.9 in 2014. On average there were 12,093 hospitalizations each year due to motor vehicle traffic-related injuries, hospitalizing 61.5 of every 100,000 New Yorkers. The rates were highest for males and New Yorkers ages 20-24.

How are the collision rates in NYC?

From January to June 2019, a total of 1667 people lost their lives due to vehicle collisions in New York City. Each silhouete in the circle symbolizes 2.5 people.

Pedestrians

Cyclists

Drivers

The accidents were also fatal for 610 drivers. According to NYPD data, the stated leading cause of collisions in NYC is driver inattentiveness
